About Me

CBT, short for Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, is one of the most effective treatments for people with anxiety or depression. CBT provides you with simple, practical techniques for coping with negative thoughts, and altering maladaptive behaviors. The treatment is designed to be time-limited - most patients meet with their therapist between 12 and 16 times as they learn to apply CBT techniques effectively. During treatment, homework is frequently assigned so that you can apply what you have learned in therapy. In particular, your therapist may ask you to keep a record of your thoughts so that you can practice challenging negative and distorted thoughts. You may also be asked to keep track of your daily activities, so that you can see how your mood is affected by your behaviors. This is where CBT Toolkit comes in. CBT Toolkit will allow you to easily keep track of your thoughts and daily activities. The web application will include both a thought diary and an activity diary, which will allow you to easily monitor the progress of your treatment.

Meet the Creator

My name is Ben Earle and I'm the developer of CBT Toolkit. I've suffered from depression and anxiety since high school. I've been lucky enough to have the support of wonderful friends and family who have helped me through some difficult times. Now I'd like to pay it forward by creating a tool that can help other people who struggle with these illnesses. I've found the methods of CBT extremely useful in my own life, when coping with setbacks and negative emotions. I hope that you will find CBT useful too, and that this web application can help you learn to cope with your illness. Please let me know if you have any questions!
